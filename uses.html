An AI Virtual Mouse uses computer vision and AI technologies to allow users to control the mouse pointer and perform actions like clicking, scrolling, and dragging — without touching a physical mouse. Here are some common and practical uses:

Accessibility for Disabled Users

Enables people with motor impairments to control computers using head movements, eye tracking, hand gestures, or voice. Improves digital accessibility and independence.

Smart Workstations

In modern workplaces, especially in design, editing, or presentation roles, users can benefit from gesture or voice-based control. Can be integrated into standing desks or smart office systems.

Educational Tools

Great for interactive learning, especially for children or people with disabilities. Can be used in STEM education to demonstrate AI and HCI (Human-Computer Interaction) concepts.

Presentation & Remote Control

Presenters can control slides using hand gestures or head movements. Works well for online teaching or virtual meetings.
